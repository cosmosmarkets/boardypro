# Boardy Pro Landing Page: Design Brief

The working brief for the Pro landing page. Status: direction locked, not yet built. The detailed system spec is [06-stage-and-graph-spec.md](06-stage-and-graph-spec.md); the cluster-by-cluster content is [07-cluster-flow.md](07-cluster-flow.md).

## The big idea

**Connector becomes closer, told as a living network you travel through.** Free Boardy makes the intro. Pro makes the deal happen. The whole page is one connected node-edge graph. You scroll it left to right, and the direction means something: rightward is intro to closed deal. You watch your own graph come alive as Boardy wires you into proof, a pipeline, a room full of investors and notable people, and finally closes the deal so your node lights up.

Boardy is the superconnector, so the page behaves like a network. The product is the metaphor.

## Visual direction

- **The page is a graph, not a stack of sections.** Nodes and brass edges on a cool near-black stage, traveled horizontally with gentle magnetic snaps onto each stop.
- **Boardy is a quiet left anchor**, not a centered monument and not a re-introduction (boardy.ai already fronts him). His **brushed-brass edges fan rightward** across every screen, so everything you meet visibly traces back to him. He bookends: present at the entry, returns at the close when your node connects. Asset: `golden boardy pro.png`, blue face kept intact.
- **Cool, near-black background.** Non-negotiable with brass: it glows on cool dark and dies on warm or light backgrounds.
- **Warm-white light at the bookends** only (entry and close), marking where Boardy stands. Never peach, never saturated.
- **"Your graph":** a represented "You" node, dim and grey at the center-edge, that gains a connection and a little more light at each cluster and ends fully lit Boardy-blue and clickable. Real logos and named investors in the outer ring for aspiration; archetypal role-labels near you for the personal pull.

## The deal-track spine (wayfinding)

A bottom spine with a playhead that slides right as you scroll. Stops: `Intro -> Proof -> Pipeline -> Differentiator -> $8M round -> Network -> Close`. It answers how far am I and what is coming. No minimap (horizontal motion is the map). A persistent "Talk to Boardy" stays docked so the exit is always one tap away.

## Four locked motion principles

1. **Edges draw on arrival.** Boardy's edges animate being drawn to each cluster's nodes as you land. You watch him wire you in.
2. **Your node comes alive.** Dim and grey at the left, fully lit blue and clickable at the close. Clicking it is the phone field.
3. **The playhead is the line between done and possible.** Edges behind it are solid brass (made); edges ahead are dim and dashed (potential). Scrolling right converts possible into done.
4. **The differentiator is a dying edge.** "The follow-up is where deals die, and where I win" shown literally: a stalled edge flatlines and fades, then Boardy's pulse revives it.

## The peak

**The $8M self-loop.** Boardy's edge loops back into himself, the proof he closed his own round on his own platform. The playhead parks on it. The gut-punch no competitor can fake.

## The cluster flow (full content in 07)

1. **Intro** (entry, far left). Locked headline carries the meaning at t=0. Boardy anchors, edges fan right, your node sits dim.
2. **Proof.** The real platform numbers (166K spoken with, 110K intros, $63B capital, 17h match) as stat-nodes. Kicker: "And that was just me making the intros. Now I stick around to close them."
3. **Pipeline.** The five-step deal mechanism threaded as a path of nodes Boardy runs you through.
4. **Differentiator.** The dying-edge moment. Boardy follows up as himself, so you never look desperate.
5. **$8M round.** The self-loop peak. "I raised my own funding round. On myself." Led by Creandum.
6. **Network.** Your graph blooms. Outer ring of real logos and named investors (the bulk), inner ring of archetypal role-labels. "The room I get you into."
7. **Close.** Boardy returns, your node goes fully live and clickable, the phone field sits with him. "One closed deal pays for a decade of me." $100/mo, free first year.

## Guardrails

- **The horizontal scroll must feel as good as native vertical scroll.** Magnetic snap, keyboard nav, deep links, a draggable scrubber. If the scrub feels like a gimmick, fix it or escalate.
- **Build the vertical fallback first.** Reduced motion and mobile collapse to a clean vertical version of the same clusters. That vertical version is the accessible, legible source of truth.
- **First paint sells before interaction.** The headline carries the message before anyone scrolls. Do not make the entry a puzzle.
- **Let the persona do the talking.** First person, Boardy, a little cheeky. Never corporate.
- **One core message:** connector becomes closer. Everything supports the one pivot.
- **No implied endorsement.** Real logos and named investors are "the rooms I can get you into", not "these people vouch for you". Inner labels stay archetypal. Verify the list before printing.
- **Gold needs a dark stage.** Cool near-black, always.
- **No em dashes** in any copy.

## The offer (confirmed)

- The offer is **live.**
- **Founding members** (the original first 5,000) are **free for life.** That window is closed.
- **New signups now get Pro free for one year**, then $100/mo.
- Legitimate urgency: "founding-member free-for-life is gone, but you can still get a year free." Demoted to a single supporting line at the Close, never the headline.
