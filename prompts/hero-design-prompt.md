# Design Prompt: Boardy Pro Hero (for claude.ai/design)

Paste the prompt below into claude.ai/design. **Attach `golden boardy pro.png`** (the brushed-brass Boardy) when you do, and reference it as the hero character. If you cannot attach it, the prompt describes him well enough to approximate.

---

Design the **hero section** of a landing page for **Boardy Pro**, the premium tier of Boardy, an AI "superconnector" that calls you, learns what you are building, and makes warm introductions. Pro is the upgrade where Boardy stops at introductions and starts closing deals. This hero is a launch moment. It should feel premium, confident, and characterful, like a flagship product reveal, not a generic SaaS template. The brand voice is "half OpenAI, half Pixar": frontier tech with a warm, playful character.

**The character (attached image, use it as the centerpiece):** Boardy is a figure with a brushed-brass cardboard-box head, a friendly blue smiley face and two blue dot eyes on the box, wearing a navy suit, white shirt, and red tie. The brass box is the premium signal. Do not recolor him. He is the hero, the single most saturated and eye-catching thing in the frame.

**Composition: a centered monument.** Everything is centered on the vertical axis. An enormous word "PRO" is set behind the character, rendered in the same brushed brass, low contrast so it reads as a backdrop rather than competing for attention. Boardy stands in front of and overlapping the PRO letters, so the character and the typography lock together as one object. The headline sits cleanly below him.

**The stage:** a cool, near-black background (deep charcoal with a slight blue-slate undertone, around #0B0E14), with a barely-there cooler lift behind the PRO word. Keep the background cool. Warmth anywhere in the background would mute the brass and ruin the effect.

**The signature element, "the arc":** at the base of the hero, the dark stage ends in a shallow, wide convex curve, like a gentle horizon bulging downward with its apex centered directly under Boardy. The edge of this curve is crisp, and a thin brass keyline (a hairline of glowing brass) rides along the top of it. Behind and just above this arc, a soft warm-white spotlight glows, hottest directly behind Boardy and falling off toward the left and right edges so the sides sink back into the dark. The effect: Boardy is standing center-stage, lit from below by a warm-white spotlight, on a brass-edged horizon. The spotlight is warm-white with the faintest gold, never peach, never orange, never saturated. It must not wash up into the PRO word.

**Palette (use exactly):**
- Stage background: #0B0E14 (cool near-black)
- Spotlight: warm-white #FCF6EA fading through soft gold #EBD9B4 to transparent
- Brass (character, PRO, arc keyline): highlights #E8C98A, mid #C99A4B, shadows #8A6A2E
- Interactive blue (the button only): #2D7DD2
- Headline text: warm off-white #F5F3EE
- Subhead text: muted cool grey #A8B0BD
- Tiny red accent (tie only): #B23A2E
Brass is for the character, the PRO word, and the arc keyline. Blue is reserved for the clickable button only. No third color.

**Typography:** a heavy, modern display grotesk (Space Grotesk or Archivo feel). Headline is large and bold, two lines, tight leading. Subhead is lighter and smaller beneath it.

**Copy (use verbatim, no em dashes anywhere):**
- Headline, two lines:
  "I'm done making intros."
  "Now I make deals happen."
- Subhead: "I pressure-test the fit, sit in on the calls, and chase the follow-ups so the deal actually closes. You take the win."
- Below the subhead, a single inline phone-number field with a button: placeholder "Your phone number", button label "Talk to Boardy" (button in blue #2D7DD2).
- Directly under the field, in small brass text: "Free for your first year."
- Under that, in tiny muted text: "I'll call you. No spam, no forms."

**Conversion detail:** the call to action is a phone-number field, not a "sign up" button, because Boardy literally calls you. Keep it to one short field plus the blue button, centered.

**Feel and finish:** premium, cinematic, lots of dark negative space, the brass catching light, the warm-white spotlight grounding the character. Subtle sense that Boardy is rising into the spotlight. Avoid clutter, avoid stock-photo gradients, avoid anything that reads as a default template. The whole frame should feel like a confident product launch.

**Top of the hero:** a minimal, transparent navigation bar with a small brass Boardy mark and "Pro" on the left. Nothing else needed.

Make it fully responsive: on mobile, stack everything tightly, character centered around 80 percent width, PRO cropping at the edges behind him, the field and button stacking full-width, the arc flattening slightly but keeping its brass keyline.
