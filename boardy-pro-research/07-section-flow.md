# Boardy Pro Landing Page: Section Flow Spec

Status: build spec for the full page below the hero. Voice is Boardy, first person, no em dashes. Reuses the palette and type system from [06-hero-spec.md](06-hero-spec.md): cool dark stage, brass for the character/headlines accents, Boardy blue for anything clickable.

## Narrative arc

Hero (what's new) → Proof bar (it's real) → Transformation (what changed) → How it works (the pipeline) → Why it's different (follows up as himself) → Proof it works (raised its own round) → The network (who you reach) → Why it exists (mission) → Close (offer + CTA).

Each section earns the next. We open with attitude, prove velocity early, explain the mechanism, defend the one objection, land the killer proof point, show the network, add the human why, then ask for the signup.

## Section 1 — Proof bar
**Purpose:** Immediate credibility. Cash the launch velocity before the visitor can get skeptical.

**Purpose (revised):** Establish the foundation Pro is built on. These are the real numbers Boardy already racked up just making intros. Then allude to Pro: that was the warm-up, closing is the next act.

**Layout:** Thin full-width strip directly under the hero. Dark bg continues. 4 stats in a row (2x2 on mobile), brass numbers, muted labels. A short Pro-forward kicker line under the row.

**Content (4 stats, live from boardy.ai, rounded down):**
- `166,000+` people I've spoken with
- `110,000+` introductions I've made
- `$63B+` in capital introduced
- `17h` average time to match

(Exact live values as of last scrape: 166,263 spoken with, 114,627 intros, $63B capital, 17h match. Round down for honesty and longevity. Pull live if the build can; these labels mirror the boardy.ai stat block.)

**Kicker line, centered under the row, in voice (this is the Pro allusion):**
"And that was just me making the intros. Now I stick around to close them."

(Alt: "All of that was the warm-up. Pro is where I close.")

**Note:** The launch-velocity numbers (1M+ views, 20K comments, 5,000 spots in 2hrs, $0 ads) move out of the proof bar. Use them in Section 5 or the close as supporting social proof, not as the lead stat block.

## Section 2 — The transformation (Intros → Deals)
**Purpose:** The core idea of the whole page. What changed about Boardy. This is the concept section, give it room and air.

**Layout:** Two-panel split, or a left-to-right arrow composition.
- Left/before: "I made the intro." Smaller, muted, the old Boardy job.
- Right/after: "Now I make the deal." Brass, dominant.
- A connecting arrow or line carries the eye left to right. Consider the classic Boardy box small on the left, brass Pro box large on the right.

**Copy:**
- Section headline: "Connecting you was just the start."
- Body: "For a year, my job was the intro. I'd find the right person and get you in the room. That part still works. But the intro was never the hard part. Closing is. So now I stay in the room. From the first hello to the signed deal, I run the whole play."

---

## Section 3 — How Pro works (the pipeline)
**Purpose:** Make "I close deals" concrete. Show the actual mechanism so it's believable, not magic.

**Layout:** Horizontal timeline / stepped cards, 5 steps. Each card has a number, a short Boardy-voice line, and a one-line detail. Brass step numbers, blue connective line. On mobile, vertical stack.

**Steps (each written as Boardy speaking):**
1. **"I pressure-test the fit."** Before you spend a minute, I tell you if it's actually worth your time.
2. **"I get us in the room."** I handle the scheduling and the prep, then I show up on the call myself.
3. **"I keep us honest on the call."** I take the notes, track what was agreed, and catch what matters.
4. **"I chase the follow-up."** I nudge the stalled threads so the deal keeps moving. As myself, so you never look desperate.
5. **"I get sharper every time."** Every deal teaches me more about how you work. The next one goes smoother.

**Section headline above the steps:** "Here's how I close." or "What I actually do."

## Section 4 — Why an AI doing your follow-up is the unlock (the differentiator)
**Purpose:** Defend the one real objection ("isn't it weird/spammy?") and turn it into the single strongest feature. This is the wedge, give it its own section.

**Layout:** Single centered statement section, generous whitespace. A short, punchy claim with a brief unpack. Maybe a small two-line "you vs me" contrast graphic.

**Copy:**
- Headline: "The follow-up is where deals die. And where I win."
- Body: "When you chase a thread, you look desperate. When I chase it, I'm just doing my job. I follow up as me, Boardy, not as a ghostwriter behind your name. So the deal keeps its momentum and you keep your leverage. That's the difference between an intro and an outcome."

## Section 5 — Proof it works: I raised my own round
**Purpose:** The strongest, most underused proof point on the entire page. The agent used its own product to raise its own money. Nothing else converts like this.

**Layout:** Feature spotlight. Dark, cinematic. Big brass stat, short story, a backing logo or two (Creandum). Could be a pull-quote treatment.

**Copy:**
- Eyebrow (small, this one earns an eyebrow): "Not a hypothetical."
- Headline: "I raised my own funding round. On myself."
- Body: "I didn't just make intros for other founders. I worked my own network, found the right investors, and closed an $8M round led by Creandum. If I can do it for me, I can do it for you."
- Stat callout: `$8M` raised by the agent, on the agent.

## Section 6 — The network (who you actually reach)
**Purpose:** Substantiate that the connections are worth $100/mo. Quality of network = quality of deals.

**Layout:** Logo wall + live metrics. Brass-on-dark logos. Metrics in a row above or below.

**Content:**
- Logos: a16z, Sequoia, Anthropic, OpenAI, Nvidia, Google, xAI, Tesla, HF0, AI Fund.
- Metrics (to avoid repeating the proof bar, lead the network angle here, e.g. capital introduced `$63B+` as the headline number since it speaks to deal quality, not just volume).
- Headline: "The room I get you into." or "Founders, investors, and operators. The people who move things."

---

## Section 7 — Why I exist (the human beat)
**Purpose:** One sincere moment under the cheek. Gives the playful brand emotional weight and separates it from "just another AI tool." Short. Don't oversell it.

**Layout:** Quiet, centered, text-forward. Less brass shine, more calm. Founder attribution.

**Copy:**
- Headline: "AI should make us more connected. Not less."
- Body: "Most tech pulls people apart. I was built to do the opposite. To put you in front of the people who change what's possible for you, and to get those deals over the line. Real relationships, real outcomes."
- Attribution line: "The reason we built Boardy. Andrew D'Souza, founder. Built by the team that deployed $5B at Clearco."

---

## Section 8 — The close (offer + pricing + final CTA)
**Purpose:** Convert. Restate the live offer and the velocity, make the ask.

**Layout:** Full-bleed, brass Boardy returns (smaller than hero), big CTA, the offer framed as scarcity. Cool dark + floor-glow callback to the hero so the page bookends.

**Copy (value-led, not scarcity-led):**
- Headline: "One closed deal pays for a decade of me."
- Subhead: "Pro is $100 a month. Your first year is free. I'll have closed something worth a lot more than that long before the bill ever shows up."
- CTA: "Talk to Boardy"
- Microcopy under CTA: "Give me your number. I'll take it from there."
- Founding-member line (small, secondary, one line only): "Founding members locked me in free for life. That window's closed, but the free year isn't."

**Why this close works:** leads with value (the $100/mo is trivial against one deal) and the free-first-year offer. The founding-member detail is demoted to a single supporting line for light FOMO, not the headline. The ask is a phone number, matching Pro's real onboarding.

## Section count and rhythm

8 sections + hero + footer. Rhythm alternates: loud (hero) → fast (proof bar) → conceptual (transformation) → detailed (pipeline) → sharp (differentiator) → big proof (own round) → broad (network) → quiet (mission) → loud close. Avoid two heavy text sections back to back.

## Build order suggestion

Hero first (already spec'd), then Section 2 (transformation) and Section 5 (raised own round) since those are the two highest-impact, most distinctive sections. The rest can follow.
