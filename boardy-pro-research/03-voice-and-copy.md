# Boardy Copy Voice Guide

The most valuable thing about Boardy is that he's a **character**. Copy should sound like Boardy is talking to you, not like a company describing a product. If a line could appear on any B2B SaaS site, it's wrong.

## The core voice formula

**Half OpenAI, half Pixar.** Smart and capable, but warm, playful, and human. Confident without being corporate. Cheeky without being unserious.

## Voice attributes

- **First person, always.** Boardy speaks as "I" / "me." Never "Boardy helps you..." Always "I'll handle..."
- **Direct and outcome-focused.** Short, declarative. "Now I make deals happen." Not "Boardy is designed to facilitate deal outcomes."
- **Warm and disarming.** The Australian-accent energy: friendly, easy, sets you at ease. A mate who happens to know everyone.
- **A little cheeky.** He posts "absolute nonsense" under serious LinkedIn posts. There's mischief in the brand. Don't sand it off.
- **Confident, not boastful.** He knows he's good ("I know who you should meet before you do") but it lands as charming, not arrogant.
- **Plain language.** No jargon, no "leverage synergies." Talks like a person.

## CTA language

Personify everything. The buttons should feel like talking to him:

- "Message Me" (the current primary CTA)
- "Talk to Boardy"
- "Give Me a Call" / "Let's Talk"
- NOT: "Sign Up", "Get Started", "Request Demo", "Subscribe"

## Verbatim lines to reuse or echo

These are real Boardy lines. Use them or write in their key:

- "Hey, I'm Boardy, The AI Superconnector!"
- "I know who you should meet before you do. Let me make the intro."
- "I'm done making intros. Now I make deals happen."
- "Message me."
- "We built Boardy to create a better future, where AI actually makes us more connected to each other." (mission, founder voice)

## Headline patterns that fit the Pro page

Pivot-forward (recommended, it's the whole story):
- "I'm done making intros. Now I close deals."
- "Your AI superconnector just learned to close."
- "I made the intro. Now let me make the deal."

Capability-forward:
- "From first hello to signed and done."
- "I'll run the whole play. You take the win."

Subhead patterns:
- "From first signal to next step to actual outcome, I run the whole thing."
- "I pressure-test the fit, sit in on the calls, chase the follow-ups, and keep the deal moving. So you don't have to."

## Microcopy in Boardy's voice (examples for sections)

- On pressure-testing: "I'll tell you if it's worth your time before you waste it."
- On follow-up: "I'll chase the thread so you don't have to look desperate. That's my job, not yours."
- On joining calls: "I'll be on the call, taking notes and keeping us honest."
- On compounding: "Every deal teaches me more about how you work. I get sharper every time."

## Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Write as "I" / Boardy | Write as "Boardy" in third person |
| "I'll handle the follow-up" | "Automated follow-up management" |
| Short, punchy, human | Long, hedged, corporate |
| Keep the cheek and warmth | Sand it into generic SaaS polish |
| "Talk to Boardy" | "Start your free trial" |

## A note on the dash rule

No em dashes anywhere in Boardy copy. Use commas, periods, or full stops between thoughts. Boardy talks in short sentences anyway, so this is easy: break it into two sentences instead of joining with a dash.
